## Cook

I would hardly consider myself a "chef" but I certainly do love to cook and bake.  

### Early Years

I helped in the kitchen growing up but mostly I was relegated to things like browning the hamburger or making sure the spaghetti didn't get mushy. I loved to help with cookies and assumed that the skill which my mom had to bake delicious, soft, and basically perfect cookies would somehow magically transfer to me.  I learned in college and the years after that this had not, in fact, been the case.  I tried and they were edible but certainly not "good" or "perfection" as I'd assumed they'd be.

### College Community

I did learn in college, however, how much I enjoyed cooking _for people_.  I had a group of friends that had regular "community meals" where we'd take turns making whatever we could buy ingredients for or knew how to make (this was in the time of the internet but before allrecipes.com had become what it is and all the recipes we could ever want were at our fingertips). While I took my turn being the lead cook I really just enjoyed the community that came from a good meal including that precious prep time in the kitchen in addition to the eating of the meal itself.  While this faded relatively quickly as friendships changed and people moved on it left an indelible mark on me that I'd learn to name later as part of my love language (yes I know that's cliché). 

### Pre-Kids Adulthood

During the early days of adulthood my wife and I split a lot of the cooking and food management needs.  We didn't do anything fancy often just eating simple recipes that we'd grown up on scheduled in a loose and often slightly varied loop.  Spending energy beyond the minimum on food preparation and refinement just wasn't where we focused and that was fine with both of us.  

### Kids

While the _immediate_ dietary needs of our children were cared for by my wife once they started to transition into purees and then solid foods I found myself really excited to dive in.  In hindsight I've connected this to that same _cooking for people_ vibe that I enjoyed so much in those college community meals but at the time it just felt like something that I could control in the swirl of chaos that is parenting an infant.  As they grow up that has matured from pureeing and freezing food for them to engaging them in the cooking process and, more recently, into falling into the deep end.  This is where I'm at now: sometimes spending hours making compound butters for morning eggs, or hand-filling agnolotti with homemade ricotta. This all brings real genuine joy to my life and I have no plans to climb back out (of the deep end, that is).
