### Nerd

#### Youngster Hack

Growing up I wasn't really sure what I wanted to do &emdash; probably like most kids after we pass the age of thinking we could be a superhero and realize that being a professional athlete or move star is quite the long shot. Got our first desktop computer for Christmas in 1993 and looking back on it I see now it was the first thing that I really felt like I loved to do and was good at. I mean it was Windows 2.5 and seeing what I could do with a `.bat` file injected into the boot sequence on a 386SX running at a cracking 16Mhz but somehow that was _so fun_.

#### Figuring Things Out

As I got into high school I found AOL Homepages and my obsession with building for the internet had begun.  I had my own site but also started trying to make a living with it by offering to make sites for my church and my dad's Goodyear business.  Neither of them turned into anything but it felt like there was something there and I couldn't shake it.

#### College

As I headed into college the two things that seemed most likely to be a successful and enjoyable job for me were something related to youth ministry and something in computers.  Both were pretty vague and I was just a little kid so I figued "why not both!"  By my sophomore year, though, it became clear that the computer science program at Augsburg just wasn't what I really wanted.  It was more hardware and robotics and less websites and development.  As such I dropped that major and started just figuring things out on my own.

#### Early Career

After graduating with my Youth & Family Ministry degree some life events caused me to move away from that as a career so I was doing some short-term work here-and-there while my wife and I got our plans figured out.  During this time I started up a side business doing custom web development and WordPress sites for non-profits and churches.  This never really became anything significant but it was a very valuable outlet for my nerd inside of me to stay engaged while I paid the bills in other ways.

#### Development for Real

FED. Gulp. Grunt. Backbone. Bootstrap.

#### Hitting My Stride

Architect. AWS. Microservices. Leading the team.
